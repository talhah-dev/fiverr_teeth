BRACES VS ALIGNERS

Two popular ways to straighten teeth

A dentist or orthodontic provider can recommend the best option based on your alignment needs, bite, and lifestyle preferences.

Comfort • Results • Fit
Traditional
Braces

Braces use brackets and wires to guide teeth into a healthier alignment. They’re a proven option for a wide range of cases.

Suitable for many alignment needs
Controlled tooth movement
Regular progress visits
Best for: broader cases, bite adjustments, or when a fixed option is preferred.
Discreet
Invisalign / Clear Aligners

Clear aligners are removable trays that gently shift teeth over time. They’re designed to be low-visibility and easy to fit into daily life.

Removable for meals and brushing
More discreet appearance
Lifestyle-friendly routine
Best for: mild-to-moderate alignment goals and patients who want a removable option.
GETTING STARTED

What an orthodontic evaluation looks like

Your provider checks alignment, bite, spacing, and overall oral health to recommend the safest and most effective plan.

Assessment

Review crowding, spacing, and bite alignment to understand your goals.

Imaging

If needed, imaging helps plan safe tooth movement and bite improvements.

Treatment plan

You’ll review a plan with estimated timeline and the best approach for your case.

Next steps

Start alignment care when you’re ready, with guidance at each stage.

Helpful note
Orthodontic plans can include bite correction, spacing improvements, and cosmetic alignment—your provider will tailor the plan to your needs.
CARE & ROUTINE

Daily habits that help your results

Whether you choose braces or aligners, consistent oral care and following provider guidance helps protect your teeth and keep progress smooth.

Clean • Consistent • Confident
Oral hygiene

Brush thoroughly and keep plaque low to support gum health during treatment.

Food choices

Your provider may suggest avoiding sticky or hard foods depending on your treatment type.

Progress visits

Regular check-ins help keep progress on track and adjust your plan when needed.

After treatment
Many plans include a retainer step to help maintain results long-term. Your provider will guide the best routine for you.
