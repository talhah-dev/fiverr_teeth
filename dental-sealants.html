WHAT ARE SEALANTS?

A thin shield for hard-to-clean chewing surfaces

Back teeth often have deep grooves where food and bacteria can hide. Sealants are applied to those grooves to help create a smoother, easier-to-clean surface.

Seals the grooves
Adds a smooth barrier where cavities often start.
Extra protection
Works alongside brushing and flossing.
Helps prevent cavities
Especially on molars and premolars.
Who can benefit most?
A dentist can recommend sealants based on risk and tooth anatomy.
Kids & teens
New molars with deep grooves can benefit from added protection.
Adults at higher risk
If you’re cavity-prone, sealants may be an extra layer of prevention.
Deep grooves
Some teeth are naturally harder to clean well.
Preventive plans
Often paired with cleanings and checkups.
Sealants are usually applied to healthy teeth without decay. Your dentist will evaluate the best option for you.
THE APPOINTMENT

A quick, comfortable application process

Sealants are typically fast to apply and don’t require drilling. A dentist cleans the tooth, prepares the surface, and sets the sealant to form a protective layer.

Simple steps • Minimal discomfort
Step 1
Clean

Teeth are cleaned and dried to prepare the surface.

Step 2
Prepare

The tooth is gently conditioned so the sealant bonds well.

Step 3
Apply

Sealant flows into the grooves to form a protective coating.

Step 4
Set

A curing light may be used to harden the sealant quickly.

RESULTS & CARE

Keep the protection working for you

Sealants are low-maintenance, but they work best when paired with regular cleanings, good brushing, and dentist checkups. Your dentist may check them during routine visits.

How to care for sealants
Simple habits that support long-term prevention.
Brush & floss daily
Sealants help, but daily care still matters.
Routine checkups
Dentists can monitor sealant wear and coverage.
Limit sugary snacks
Lowering sugar helps reduce decay risk.
Ask about fluoride
A dentist may recommend added protection if needed.
If a sealant chips or wears down, a dentist can evaluate and reapply coverage if appropriate.
