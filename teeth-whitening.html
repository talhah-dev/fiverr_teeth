WHO IS IT FOR?

Is teeth whitening right for you?

Whitening works best on natural tooth enamel stains. A dentist can evaluate sensitivity, existing dental work, and the best approach to reach your goal.

Surface stains

Common stains from coffee, tea, soda, and certain foods may respond well.

Deeper discoloration

Some internal stains may need a dentist-recommended plan for best results.

Existing dental work

Crowns, veneers, and fillings don’t whiten the same way. A dentist can advise on tone matching.

Sensitivity

If your teeth are sensitive, a dentist can recommend a safer, more comfortable option.

Pro tip
A cleaning before whitening can help improve results by removing surface buildup and stains.
FAQ

Common questions about whitening

Here are answers to common topics like sensitivity, duration, and keeping results longer.

How long do results last?
Results vary by habits and stain sources. Many people maintain brightness longer with good hygiene and limiting staining foods/drinks.
Will whitening cause sensitivity?
Some people experience temporary sensitivity. A dentist can recommend formulas or schedules designed to improve comfort.
Does whitening work on crowns or veneers?
Whitening mainly affects natural tooth enamel. A dentist can help you plan the best path if you have dental restorations.
How often can I whiten?
Frequency depends on the method and your sensitivity. Follow dentist instructions to avoid overuse and protect enamel.
